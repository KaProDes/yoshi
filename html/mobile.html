Chrome is literally Hitler

And why you should use Brave instead
Kapil Deshmukh | 3rd June 2019 | 22:10:52

So, the other day, in one of my vagabond pursuits, I tried out the desktop version of Google Chrome.
I fired up my trusty Firefox and in true windows 10 dunce style downloaded an exe (Package management in Linux is so much better).
After my endeavors at donkle-dink were over and my copy of Chrome was loaded up, I realized how unrefined and trashy it looked. I mean in the sad-boi days of Internet Explorer, chrome was a good sent; quick, refined and snappy. But, over the years, it has become a bloated mess with Google's telemetry being a cherry on top of the shit-cake that has become of Google Chrome.
"Look how they massacred my boy," I would've exclaimed; but I realized that Chrome was hardly my jam and I cared two twinkle-tubs about it.
Google chrome has become bad to the point that it has become a meme. It suffers from memory-hogging issues and eats away your system resources like there's no tomorrow. Not to mention how it fools you by feeling fast rather than being fast. It is a fake it till you make it, situation like you've never seen before.
As the gate keeper of the chromium project and the brand ambassador of the chromium world, Chrome gets a big L in my arrogant, entitled opinion.
I had heard about the Chromium Project and how it was a God-sent in the noble mission to have universally compatible browser extensions. So in true gobshite fashion I spent the afternoon testing out different Chromium based browsers - Epic is a dream come true for privacy aficionados, Vivaldi just screams customization and stability - rightfully so since it is made by Jon von Tetzchner of the ex-Opera fame. Furthermore, Blisk is a developer's best friend with its plethora of built in tools. Any web-dev should jump on the Blisk gravy-train ASAP, IMO.
But, none of them impressed me as Brave did. Brave was faster, stable and chrome-esqe without the cheap feel that one associates chrome with nowadays. For starters, Brave are the Godfathers of the Basic Attention Token aka BAT project which you can know more about here.
But, the TLDR; version of it is - BAT is project that rewards users to voluntarily view Ads in return for cash (upto $200 a year).
Brave browser also has a novel approach when it comes to cookies, ads and trackers; something that we've just accepted as normal - a horrible status quo that ought to change. Instead, it simply takes all those ads, cookies and trackers; shoves them into a dumpster and lights a fire over it - only to light a fat bong to make fat clouds in celebration of internet privacy.
I bet they have a better, more sophisticated explanation of that; but its a BRAVE step in the right direction.
In my testing, I found nearly everything 1.5 times faster than chrome; makes sense considering they has a stripped down approach as compared to our Nazi Chrome overlords.
Now if my privacy and speed arguments don't convert you or at least persuade you to try brave out - here is a handy list of white-knighting arguments

  • Faster more secure, no tracking in the traditional sense
  • Chrome/Chromium extension support, so you don't miss out on your kitten homepage extension(looking at you, Mix, wink-wink)
  • TOR browsing built in - Nuff said
  • Underdog : 5.5 Million active users as against Chrome's massive 1 Billion - Don't be a normie
  • No questions asked ad blocking from the get-go | BAT program to volunteer to view Ads in exchange for some cash
  • Built on the philosophy that just because it is free, doesn't mean that the user is a marketable product for ad revenue
  • Brainchild of Brendan Eich (js creator, father of modern internet) and Brian Bondy (Co-founder of Mozilla)
I know Evangelism does not work and so I am simply here to make this PSA.
Give Brave a shot, its a Coderman64 certified product! Download here
